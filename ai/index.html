THE LEARNING JOURNEY

One complete project, built one step at a time.

Each phase turns an idea into something students can see, refine, and explain. Progress is structured, visible, and connected to the final video.

  1. 01
    5 MEETINGS

    Story Foundations

    Choose an idea, shape a clear concept, write a short script, and plan the scenes.

  2. 02
    4 MEETINGS

    Visual Production

    Write stronger prompts, create every scene, and revise the visuals for consistency.

  3. 03
    4 MEETINGS

    Video & Editing

    Record the narration, arrange scenes, and add purposeful movement, sound, and text.

  4. 04
    3 MEETINGS

    Finalization & Showcase

    Review quality and ethics, export the final video, and present the creative process.

WHAT STUDENTS WILL CREATE

Students don’t just learn AI. They complete a project.

Every deliverable becomes part of one coherent video—from the first idea to the final showcase.

  1. 01

    A Developed Story Concept

    A clear title, message, character, setting, and short script.

  2. 02

    A Structured Storyboard

    Three to five planned scenes with visuals and narration.

  3. 03

    Consistent Visual Scenes

    A selected and revised visual for every part of the story.

  4. 04

    One Presentation-Ready Video

    An edited final video with narration, movement, text, and reflection.

HOW EVERY MEETING WORKS

60 active, focused minutes—always moving the project forward.

Students connect with the topic, watch a clear demonstration, try it together, then spend most of the meeting creating independently. Every session produces real progress toward the final video.

  1. 5 MIN01

    Opening

    Connect the theme to students’ experiences.

  2. 8 MIN02

    Teacher Demo

    See the process through one clear example.

  3. 7 MIN03

    Guided Practice

    Try the concept together with support.

  4. 35 MIN04

    Individual Creation

    Build the next part of each student’s project.

  5. 5 MIN05

    Save & Reflect

    Organize files and identify the next improvement.

MORE THAN A VIDEO

There is a thoughtful process behind the final project.

The finished video makes learning visible, but the lasting value is the way students learn to develop, evaluate, improve, and explain their creative decisions.

  • Storytelling & Idea DevelopmentTurn a broad topic into one clear, meaningful story.
  • Visual CommunicationChoose and refine visuals that support the message.
  • Structured Creative WorkflowMove from idea to final output through a repeatable process.
  • Responsible Use of AIReview privacy, safety, accuracy, and respectful content.
  • Presentation & ReflectionExplain the work, receive feedback, and identify growth.
